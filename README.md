# ORDER-DATA-ANALYSIS
This project analyzes order data to understand sales performance, product behavior, and business trends. Using Microsoft Excel, pivot tables, and dashboards, the analysis provides insights that support better decision-making in sales, inventory management, and business strategy.

### Project Overview
This project involves analyzing transactional order data to evaluate overall business performance. The dataset contains information on orders, products, categories, quantities, and sales values. The analysis focuses on identifying trends, top-performing products, and areas requiring improvement.

---
### Project Scope

The scope of this project includes:
-	Sales performance analysis
-	Product and product category evaluation
-	Sales trend analysis over time
- Identification of top-selling and least-selling products

Predictive modeling and external datasets are outside the scope of this project.

---
### Project Objectives
The ojectives of this analysis are to:

- Analyze overall sales and profitability
- Identify top-performing and low-performing products
- Evaluate product category performance
- Analyze sales trends over time
- Support informed business decision-making

---
### Expected Outcome

The expected outcomes include:
- Clear understanding of overall sales performance
-	Identification of products driving the most sales
-	Visibility into sales trends and patterns
-	Actionable insights for business improvement

### Document Purpose

This documentation serves as a guide for project stakeholders, providing insights into the project's objectives, data sources, data analysis, visualizations, and other relevant information.

Stakeholders Who Can Benefit

Business Owners / Management

Sales & Operations Teams

Strategy & Planning Teams

### Data Source
The dataset used for this project was downloaded from **Kaggle**, a publicly available data platform commonly used for data analysis practice and learning.  
The dataset contains order-level transactional data and was used strictly for educational and portfolio purposes.

---

### Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel  
- Pivot Tables  
- Charts  
- Interactive Dashboards  

---

## Data Cleaning and Processing
- Removed unnecessary and empty columns  
- Checked for missing and duplicate records  
- Ensured correct formatting for dates and numerical values  
- Verified calculated fields such as revenue, profit, and quantity  

---

## Data Analysis
The analysis was performed using pivot tables and Excel formulas to:
- Calculate key performance indicators (KPIs)
- Analyze sales by product and category
- Identify top and bottom performing products
- Examine sales trends across time periods

## Key Insights
- Technology products generate the highest revenue and profit
- A small number of products contribute significantly to total revenue
- Sales performance shows clear seasonal and time-based trends
- Some products consistently underperform and may require review

---

## Recommendations
- Focus inventory and marketing on high-performing products
- Review pricing or promotion strategies for low-performing products
- Align staffing and inventory planning with peak sales periods
- Use category performance insights to guide business strategy

---

## Conclusion
This project demonstrates how Microsoft Excel can be effectively used to analyze order data and generate meaningful business insights. The dashboards and analysis support data-driven decision-making in sales, operations, and strategic planning.
